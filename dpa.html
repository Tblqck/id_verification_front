Data Processing Addendum

Last updated 9 July 2026. This DPA describes how idntory, Inc. ("processor") handles personal data on behalf of a business customer ("controller") when the customer submits end-user data — documents, selfies, liveness frames — to idntory for verification.

1. Roles

For verification data submitted through the API, the customer is the data controller and idntory acts as data processor, processing personal data only to deliver the verification result and only on the customer's documented instructions (i.e. the API request itself).

2. Subject matter & duration

Processing covers identity document images, selfies, liveness frames and the data extracted from them (MRZ fields, biometric match scores, verdicts), for the duration of the customer's use of the service plus the configured retention window described in our Compliance page.

3. Security measures

idntory applies technical and organisational measures appropriate to the sensitivity of identity and biometric data, including:

  • Encryption of data in transit (HTTPS/TLS) for all API and dashboard traffic.
  • Access to verification data restricted to a permissioned admin dashboard, not raw file exports.
  • Configurable, per-deployment retention windows for documents and biometric frames.

Formal certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001) are on our roadmap, not yet complete — see Compliance for the current state.

4. Subprocessors

idntory uses a limited set of infrastructure and email-delivery subprocessors necessary to run the service. We'll provide an up-to-date list on request, and will not add a new subprocessor that materially changes how end-user data is handled without notifying affected customers.

5. Sub-processing on your behalf

idntory will not disclose personal data processed on a customer's behalf to third parties except subprocessors under this DPA, or where required by law — in which case we'll notify the customer unless legally prohibited from doing so.

6. Assistance & deletion

We'll reasonably assist customers in responding to data subject requests relating to data processed through idntory, and will delete or return verification data at the end of the retention window or on request, subject to any legal retention obligations.

7. Signing a DPA for your account

Enterprise customers needing an executed DPA (including EU Standard Contractual Clauses where relevant) as part of a procurement or compliance process should contact us — we're glad to work through it alongside your legal team.
